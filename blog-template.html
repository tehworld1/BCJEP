New Environmental Science Curriculum Approved

Published on March 8, 2025 | By BCJEP Team

The Bethlehem Area School District has approved our proposal for a comprehensive environmental science curriculum. This groundbreaking decision marks a significant step forward in our mission to integrate climate education into our schools. After months of advocacy, research, and collaboration with educators, students, and community members, we are thrilled to see this initiative come to fruition.

A Comprehensive Approach to Climate Education

The new curriculum adopts a multidisciplinary approach to environmental science education. It will be integrated across various subjects, ensuring that students understand climate-related issues from multiple perspectives—scientific, social, economic, and ethical.

Key components of the new curriculum include:

  • Hands-on field experiences and data collection
  • Community-based projects addressing local environmental challenges
  • Integration of climate science into core subjects
  • Focus on solutions and climate justice principles
  • Development of critical thinking skills through case studies

Implementation Timeline

The curriculum will begin rolling out in the 2025-2026 academic year, with full implementation expected by 2027. Teacher training workshops will commence this summer, providing educators with the resources and knowledge they need to effectively deliver this new content.

"This represents a significant shift in how we approach science education in the district. By focusing on environmental science, we're not just teaching concepts—we're preparing students to be informed citizens and problem-solvers for the challenges their generation will face." — Dr. Maria Rodriguez, BASD Curriculum Director

Community Support and Collaboration

This achievement would not have been possible without the passionate advocacy of students, parents, teachers, and community members. The school board vote (7-2 in favor) reflected the strong community support for this initiative.

Local environmental organizations, including the Lehigh Valley Sustainability Network and Bethlehem Environmental Advisory Council, will continue to partner with the district to provide resources, guest speakers, and real-world learning opportunities for students.

Next Steps

While this approval represents a major milestone, our work continues. The BCJEP team will now focus on supporting curriculum development, coordinating teacher training, and ensuring that implementation proceeds smoothly.

We invite community members interested in supporting this initiative to contact us regarding volunteer opportunities, resource sharing, or other forms of collaboration.

About BCJEP Team

The Bethlehem Climate Justice Education Project team consists of students, teachers, and community members dedicated to advancing climate education in local schools.
